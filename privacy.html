Privacy Policy

We collect as little as possible. Here's exactly what we do and don't know about you.

Last updated: July 1, 2026

1. Overview

GearsVPN is built on a simple principle: we can't hand over data we don't have. Our service is architected from the ground up to operate without logging user activity, identifying browsing behaviour, or retaining information about your sessions.

This policy explains exactly what we collect, why we collect it, how we protect it, and — crucially — everything we deliberately do not collect. We aim to be plain-language and comprehensive so you never have to guess what happens to your information.

2. What We Collect

We collect the absolute minimum required to operate and maintain your account. Nothing more.

What we do collect

✓ Email address — used to create and identify your account, deliver billing receipts, and send critical security notices.
✓ Payment reference — Stripe provides only a transaction confirmation and last-4-digit card reference. We never see or store your full card number.
✓ Service emails — account creation confirmation, billing receipts, and security alerts.

What we never collect

✕ Your real IP address
✕ IP addresses of servers you visit
✕ Your DNS queries
✕ Websites or content you access
✕ Connection timestamps or session durations
✕ Bandwidth used per session
✕ Device type or OS details
✕ Your location (beyond what you voluntarily provide)

3. What We DON'T Collect

Our VPN infrastructure is explicitly designed so that the following data is never generated, transmitted to our systems, or stored anywhere on our servers:

  • —Your real IP address or the origin IP of your connection
  • —The IP addresses of any websites, services, or servers you connect to while using GearsVPN
  • —Your DNS queries — we operate a private, non-logging DNS resolver
  • —The websites, pages, or any content you access or download
  • —Connection timestamps, session start times, or session durations
  • —The amount of bandwidth you consume per session or per day
  • —Your device type, operating system, or browser fingerprint
  • —Your physical location beyond what you voluntarily provide during sign-up

Because this data is never created on our end, it cannot be subpoenaed, stolen, or disclosed — even under legal compulsion.

4. How We Use Your Data

We use the limited data we collect for exactly two purposes:

  • —Email address: to deliver your account credentials, billing receipts, and critical security notices (e.g., a password-reset request you initiated).
  • —Payment reference: to verify your subscription status and unlock service access. No other use.

We do not sell, rent, trade, or share your data with advertisers, data brokers, analytics companies, or any third parties for commercial purposes. Your information is used only to provide you with the service you paid for.

5. Third-Party Services

We use a minimal number of third-party providers, each selected for their strong privacy posture. We do not use analytics SDKs, tracking pixels, ad networks, or session-recording tools of any kind.

Stripe — payment processing. Stripe handles all card data in accordance with PCI DSS Level 1 standards. We receive only a transaction confirmation and a last-4-digits reference. View Stripe's privacy policy at privacy.stripe.com.
Mailgun — transactional email delivery. Used solely to dispatch account-creation confirmations, billing receipts, and security alerts. Mailgun does not receive any VPN usage data.

6. Cookies

We use only the cookies strictly necessary for your account session to function. Specifically, a single encrypted session cookie is set when you log in to the GearsVPN dashboard; it is deleted when you log out or your session expires.

We do not use:

  • —Tracking or analytics cookies
  • —Third-party advertising cookies
  • —Persistent fingerprinting cookies
  • —Any cookies served by ad networks or marketing platforms

Because we don't use non-essential cookies, you will not see a cookie-consent banner on our site.

7. Data Security

We apply industry-standard and beyond-standard security practices to protect the limited data we do hold:

All data in transit is encrypted via TLS 1.3 — older, weaker cipher suites are explicitly disabled.
Account passwords are hashed using bcrypt with a cost factor appropriate for current hardware — we never store plaintext or reversibly-encrypted passwords.
Our database is encrypted at rest using AES-256, with keys managed separately from the data store.
We commission regular third-party security audits and penetration tests. Results and remediation timelines are reviewed by our engineering team upon receipt.

Despite these measures, no system is perfectly immune. If we become aware of a breach affecting your personal data, we will notify you by email within 72 hours of discovery, in line with GDPR Article 34 requirements.

8. Your Rights

Whether you are located in the European Economic Area (GDPR) or California (CCPA), you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • —Right to access: request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • —Right to rectification: ask us to correct any inaccurate data.
  • —Right to erasure: request deletion of all personal data associated with your account.
  • —Right to portability: receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • —Right to object: object to any processing you believe is not justified.

To exercise any of these rights, email us at privacy@gearsvpn.com. We respond to all requests within 30 days of receipt.

You may also delete your account at any time directly from your dashboard. Doing so immediately triggers a purge of all personal data associated with your account within 48 hours.

9. Data Retention

We retain your data only as long as necessary:

  • —Account data (email address) is retained while your subscription is active and deleted within 48 hours of account deletion or subscription termination.
  • —Payment references (last-4-digit card references and transaction IDs) are retained for 7 years to satisfy tax compliance and accounting obligations. This data is stored on Stripe's infrastructure — not on our servers — and is the minimum required under applicable financial regulations.

We do not retain any data beyond these windows. There are no "soft deletes" that keep your information in hidden archives after you leave.
